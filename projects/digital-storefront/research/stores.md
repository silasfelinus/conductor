# Store/platform research shortlist

Date: 2026-06-26
Task: digital-storefront/t-001
Status: draft for Silas review

## Scope

This research covers candidate stores/platforms for the currently approved product types only:

- `pod-text-art` — print-on-demand text/art designs
- `pdf-rpg` — digital tabletop RPG PDFs
- `pdf-manual` — digital instruction manuals
- `pdf-coloring` — digital coloring books
- `stl-3d` — 3D printable STL files

No accounts were created, no listings were published, and no paid services were changed.

## Executive recommendation

Start with a low-risk, three-lane setup:

1. **Gumroad** as the general direct-sales hub for PDFs and small digital products.
2. **itch.io** as the indie/community storefront for RPG PDFs, zines, coloring PDFs, bundles, and experiments.
3. **Printful or Printify connected later to Etsy or a direct shop** for print-on-demand once designs are chosen.

For STL files, use **Cults** or **MyMiniFactory** as the first dedicated 3D-file marketplace, with **Printables/Thingiverse** treated as free-sample discovery channels rather than primary storefronts.

Avoid starting with too many channels. The best first move is to approve one digital platform and one POD/STL platform, publish drafts internally, then decide where Silas wants accounts and branding created.

## Recommended shortlist by product type

### Print-on-demand text/art designs (`pod-text-art`)

**Primary recommendation:** Printful or Printify as fulfillment, with a storefront chosen later.

- Use **Printful** when the priority is predictable quality, smoother setup, and fewer provider decisions.
- Use **Printify** when the priority is margin testing and choosing cheaper/faster providers.
- Use **Redbubble** for quick marketplace experiments, especially stickers, simple merch, and visual/text designs.
- Use **Etsy** later if Silas wants marketplace discovery and is willing to maintain listings, SEO, and fee complexity.

Suggested first action after approval: choose 3-5 candidate designs, create mockups only, and compare Printful vs Printify base cost + shipping for the same products.

### RPG PDFs (`pdf-rpg`)

**Primary recommendation:** itch.io first, DriveThruRPG second.

- **itch.io** is best for small games, zines, weird experiments, jam-style bundles, and pay-what-you-want testing.
- **DriveThruRPG** is best once a product is polished enough for a dedicated RPG marketplace.
- **DMsGuild** should be avoided unless the product deliberately uses D&D-compatible community-content rules and Silas accepts the licensing limits.

Suggested first action after approval: draft one original mini-RPG PDF concept for itch.io, with DriveThruRPG metadata held until the product is more complete.

### Instruction manuals (`pdf-manual`)

**Primary recommendation:** Gumroad first, Ko-fi as optional support layer.

- **Gumroad** is the cleanest fit for direct links, manuals, guides, and bundled downloads.
- **Ko-fi Shop** is good if the manual is part of a creator/supporter ecosystem.
- **Etsy** can work for printable planners, templates, and craft-style manuals, but it is a more competitive SEO marketplace.

Suggested first action after approval: define one manual category, then create a Gumroad-ready product page draft and PDF outline.

### Coloring PDFs (`pdf-coloring`)

**Primary recommendation:** Gumroad + itch.io; Etsy later if the product has a clear printable/search niche.

- **Gumroad** handles direct sales and bundles well.
- **itch.io** is surprisingly good for indie/zine/coloring oddities, especially themed packs.
- **Etsy** is useful if the product targets highly searchable printable niches, but competition is intense.

Suggested first action after approval: make one themed PDF coloring pack concept and draft store copy for both Gumroad and itch.io.

### STL files (`stl-3d`)

**Primary recommendation:** Cults for first marketplace test, MyMiniFactory for polished collections.

- **Cults** appears best for broad paid STL marketplace discovery.
- **MyMiniFactory** is a strong fit for curated tabletop/hobby models and grew strategically after acquiring Thingiverse in Feb. 2026.
- **Printables/Thingiverse** should be treated as free-sample channels that link back to paid bundles elsewhere.

Suggested first action after approval: draft one STL product concept with one free sample model and one paid bundle plan.

## Decision matrix

| Need | Best platform |
| --- | --- |
| Fastest direct digital sales | Gumroad |
| Indie RPG/zine community | itch.io |
| Creator support + small shop | Ko-fi |
| Marketplace discovery for printables/POD | Etsy |
| POD fulfillment quality/control | Printful |
| POD fulfillment margin testing | Printify |
| Low-effort POD marketplace experiment | Redbubble |
| Serious RPG PDF marketplace | DriveThruRPG |
| D&D-specific community content | DMsGuild only if intentionally D&D/IP-bound |
| First paid STL marketplace | Cults |
| Polished curated STL collections | MyMiniFactory |
| Free STL samples/discovery | Printables / Thingiverse |

## Recommended first approval choice for Silas

Approve this path:

1. **Digital first:** Gumroad + itch.io.
2. **POD research next:** compare Printful vs Printify costs on a small mockup set.
3. **STL later:** evaluate Cults + MyMiniFactory once there is an actual STL concept.

Reason: this keeps the first iteration cheap, reversible, and draft-only. Digital PDFs can be created and tested without inventory, fulfillment, returns, or marketplace SEO goblins gnawing on our ankles.
